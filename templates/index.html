Soup or Dumpling?

The soup or dumpling hypothesis posits that all dishes can be classified as variations on soup or dumplings. Pizza, for example, is a deconstructed dumpling. Sometimes, however, it can be difficult to determine whether a particular dish should be classified as a soup or a dumpling. The AI below uses machine learning to classify any dish as a soup or a dumpling.

Why an AI?

The soup-dumpling hypothesis currently lacks a principled theory about what makes a dish either a soup or dumpling. To date, all theories put forth (e.g, topological agruments) have not been well accepted in the community. By training an AI against well-accepted examples, the AI should learn to generalize to other foods that are more contentious. While this does not generate a principled theory, the AI may learn to empirically draw the boundary between soups and dumplings, should such a boundary exist.
